National Parks FAQ

Arches

  • Arches National Park, located in Utah, boasts over 2,000 natural sandstone arches, formed through millions of years of erosion.
  • Delicate Arch, one of its most iconic landmarks, stands tall at 52 feet and is a popular spot for hikers.
  • The park's landscape showcases other geological formations like spires, fins, and balanced rocks, creating a surreal desert scenery.
  • Visitors can witness stunning sunrises and sunsets, transforming the park's red rock formations into vibrant shades of orange and pink.

Canyonlands

  • Canyonlands National Park, also in Utah, is a vast wilderness characterized by its dramatic canyons, mesas, and buttes carved by the Colorado River and its tributaries over millions of years.
  • The park is divided into four distinct districts: Island in the Sky, The Needles, The Maze, and the riverside districts, each offering unique landscapes and recreational opportunities.
  • Visitors can explore its rugged terrain through hiking, mountain biking, and four-wheel driving along the park's extensive trail system.
  • Canyonlands is renowned for its breathtaking vistas, especially from viewpoints like Mesa Arch and Grand View Point.

Glacier

  • Glacier National Park, nestled in Montana's Rocky Mountains, spans over a million acres of pristine wilderness.
  • It's home to over 130 named lakes, including the stunning Lake McDonald and St. Mary Lake, carved by ancient glaciers.
  • The park boasts about 700 miles of hiking trails, winding through alpine meadows, dense forests, and rugged peaks, offering visitors an array of experiences.
  • Known as the "Crown of the Continent," Glacier National Park showcases diverse wildlife, including grizzly bears, mountain goats, and elusive wolverines, within its breathtaking landscapes.

Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2018: 318,211,833.
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: More than 20,000
What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 1951.
How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 419 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
What is the largest national park site?

What do I need to know about driving off road in national parks?
Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal. Where off-road driving is allowed, the National Park Service regulates it.
